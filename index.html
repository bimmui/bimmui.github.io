Grandma once said the ocean's edge is God's scissorwork—
but I found a shell today,
its spiral a staircase for ghosts.
Each groove repeats the same story,
smaller,
until the plot dissolves into salt.

I tried to count the ridges, but my finger
always slips. The maps says this beach
is 3.7 miles long. Lies.
Every waves redraws it.

(Somewhere, a tide pools in the whorl of my ear.
I am measuring distances that refuse to converge.)

When I was seven, my brother swore
he counted every star. I didn't believe him.

Now, I work the night shift at this hotel
where numbers unpack their suitcases
and never leave.

Room π plays Hungarian jazz.
Room √2 hums a a tone that won't resolve.

I knock on doors just to hear the hollow
of forever.
My keycard flickers.
The elevator light is broken.
